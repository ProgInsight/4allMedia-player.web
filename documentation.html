Getting Started with 4allMedia Player

Welcome to 4allMedia Player! This guide will help you get started with all the essential features. Learn how to add folders, manage playlists, and configure your settings.

Quick Start

Fast Mode

  • Click "Open File" on the welcome screen, or use Quick Access if your media files are in the Music, Videos, or Downloads folder.
  • Open files directly by right-clicking a media file and selecting "Open with 4allMedia Player".
  • Drag and drop files to open them, when other media is currently playing "Not in Welcome screen".
  • Launch media from the terminal using the command: "4allMedia player" "/file/path".

Add Media Library for Quick Access and Playlists

Follow these steps to set up your media library:

  • Add folders to scan for media files
  • Create playlists to organize your content
  • Customize your player appearance and settings
  • Enable the AI widget for voice control and assistance
  • Choose your preferred media backend: VLC, MPV, or GStreamer

Each step is explained in detail in the sections below. Click on any feature in the left menu to navigate to that topic.

📌

Add Folder for Scanning

By default, 4allMedia Player automatically scans for media files in your Music and Videos folders only.

You can add custom folders to allow 4allMedia Player to scan and index additional locations for quick access.

How to Add a Folder

  • Step 1: Click on the "Folders" filter in the Welcome screen
  • Step 2: The left panel will open automatically. Look for the "+" (Add) button and click it
  • Step 3: A file browser dialog will open
  • Step 4: Navigate to the folder containing your media files
  • Step 5: Click "Select" or "Add Folder" to confirm
  • Step 6: The player will begin scanning for media files automatically

Scanning Process

Once you add a folder, the player will:

  • Recursively scan all subfolders for supported media files
  • Index file metadata (title, artist, duration, album, etc.)
  • Display progress in the status bar
  • Add discovered media to your library automatically

Tips

  • You can add multiple folders—they will all be scanned and indexed
  • Add your largest media storage folders to maximize your library
  • The scanning process runs in the background and won't interrupt playback

Create Playlist

Create new playlists to organize your media by mood, genre, artist, or any custom category you prefer.

How to Create a New Playlist

  • Step 1: Click the "+" (Add) button in the Playlists section of the left sidebar
  • Step 2: A dialog box will appear prompting you to enter the playlist name
  • Step 3: Type your desired playlist name (e.g., "Workout Mix", "Chill Vibes", "Road Trip")
  • Step 4: Click "Create" to add the new playlist
  • Step 5: Your new playlist appears in the sidebar, ready for playing or adding more content
  • TROUBLESHOOT: Once the playlist is created, it may show no content. Consider refreshing by switching to another filter (e.g., MV) and then back to the Playlist filter.

Adding Content to Your New Playlist

  • Add Files Directly: Right-click the playlist name and select "Add Files" to browse your system
  • From Library: Right-click any media file in your library and select "Add to [Playlist Name]"
  • Bulk Selection: Select multiple files, right-click on the playlist name, and add them all at once
  • From Filters: Navigate to Music, Videos, or other filters, select files, and add them to your playlist

Rename a Playlist

  • You cannot rename playlists at this time. Simply create a new one instead
  • To create a new playlist, see "Create Playlist" above

Playlist Organization Tips

  • Create playlists by genre, artist, mood, or activity for easy access.
  • Use descriptive names so you can identify playlists quickly.
  • Consider creating seasonal or event-based playlists (e.g., "Summer 2025", "Party Mix").
  • Export playlists to share with friends (supports M3U, PLS, and XSPF formats).
  • After creating a playlist, consider refreshing by switching to another filter, then back to the Playlist filter.

Delete Playlist

Remove playlists you no longer need. Deleting a playlist removes only the collection—your original media files remain safe in your library.

How to Delete a Playlist Entirely

  • Method 1 - Context Menu: Right-click the playlist name in the sidebar and select "Delete Playlist"
  • Method 2 - Delete Database: For Windows, the database is located at "C:\Users\yourname\AppData\Roaming\Player\Playlist.db" For Debian within "~/.player/playlist.db"

Deletion Confirmation

A confirmation dialog will appear asking you to confirm the deletion. This safeguard prevents accidental removal of your playlists. Click "Confirm" to proceed or "Cancel" to keep the playlist.

What Happens After Deletion

  • The playlist is permanently removed from your library
  • All media files that were in the playlist remain in your library
  • Deletion cannot be undone
  • To recover a deleted playlist, you must recreate it manually and re-add the content

Alternative: Clear Playlist Contents

If you want to keep the playlist but remove all its contents, you can do so without deleting the playlist itself:

  • Double-click the playlist to expand it and view its contents
  • Hover over an individual track
  • Right-click and choose "Remove from Playlist"
  • The playlist remains in your sidebar, empty and ready for new content
  • Bulk removal of individual playlist files is not currently supported

Important Notes

  • Clearing a playlist's contents is useful when you want to repurpose it for different media
  • Deleted playlists cannot be recovered through the application
  • Consider exporting important playlists before deletion as a backup
  • Exporting and importing playlists from the GUI is not currently supported

Playlist Management Overview

Playlists are collections of media files organized according to your preferences. Manage all your playlists from the main library view.

Main Playlist Features

  • View Playlists: All your playlists appear in the left sidebar under "Playlists"
  • Right-Click Context Menu: Right-click any playlist to access management options
  • Expand Playlist: Double-click a playlist to view its contents
  • Quick Preview: Hover over a playlist to see track count and total duration

Playlist Context Menu Options

Right-click on any playlist name in the sidebar to access these options:

  • Add Files: Browse and add media files to the selected playlist
  • Play All: Start playing all tracks in the playlist sequentially
  • Sort Files: Sort the playlist within the database based on your preference
  • Delete Playlist: Remove the playlist from your library (media files remain intact)

Adding Tracks to Playlists

There are multiple ways to add tracks to your playlists:

  • From Playlist Context Menu: Right-click the playlist name and select "Add Files"
  • From Library View: Right-click any track in Music, Videos, or other filters and select "Add to Playlist"
  • Bulk Selection: In the Playlist filter, right-click then select "Add Files" to add multiple files from your system at once
  • File Browser: Use the "Add Files" option to browse and select media from your system

Managing Tracks Within Playlists

Once you've expanded a playlist by double-clicking it:

  • View Contents: See all tracks currently in the playlist
  • Remove Tracks: Right-click any track and select "Remove from Playlist"
  • Multiple Removals: Currently not supported
  • Reorder Items: Click-and-drag reordering is not supported in the context menu. You can rely on sorting by time or name instead

Important Notes

  • Removing tracks from a playlist does not delete them from your library
  • You can add the same track to multiple playlists
  • Playlists are saved automatically within the database until you remove them

Opening Files

Opening files is what makes 4allMedia Player truly special. The player offers more than 8 different methods to open and play your media files, providing maximum flexibility and convenience.

Ways to Open Files

  • From Library Panel: Browse your scanned folders in the left panel and double-click any file to start playing. You can also use the search function to find specific media
  • AI-Assisted Search: Ask the AI assistant to search for and play media files for you
  • System File Association: Open media files directly from your system by selecting 4allMedia Player as the default application
  • Direct File Browser: Right-click on the player to access your system files, USB drives, or DVD content directly
  • Drag & Drop: Drag media files directly onto the player window when media is currently playing (not available on the welcome screen)
  • Recent Media: Access previously played files from the recent media section in the bottom-right corner
  • File Browser Button: Click the "Open File" button and browse your computer
  • Context Menu Shortcut: Right-click while media is playing and select "Open File" or "Open URL"

Opening URLs and Streams

  • Click "Open URL" at the top of the playing media interface
  • Paste YouTube, Twitch, or HTTP stream links into the URL field
  • Press Enter to start playback
  • The player supports most streaming protocols, thanks to the VLC backend
  • YouTube Direct Play: Right-click on top of any playing media, navigate to "Other," then select "YouTube" to play YouTube content directly

Recently Played Media

  • Access your recently played files from the "Recent" section in the bottom-right corner
  • Quick resume: Check the playback timer and hover over it to resume from that specific timestamp
  • Clear recent history: Select the hamburger menu in the bottom-right corner and click the trash button
  • Note: Closing and reopening the application may clear the recent play history

Keyboard Shortcuts for File Operations

  • Ctrl+O: Open file dialog (coming soon)
  • Ctrl+L: Open URL/stream (coming soon)
  • Ctrl+Q: Quit application (coming soon)

Access Settings

Customize 4allMedia Player to match your preferences through the comprehensive Settings menu. Configure appearance, themes, language, library management, and technical options to create your ideal media experience. Also you can check some shortcuts here! but editing shortcuts is current not supported

How to Open Settings

  • Main Method: Click the settings filter in the top-right corner of the filter section
  • The settings panel will open, displaying all available configuration options

Available Settings Categories

Language Settings

  • Interface Language: Select your preferred language for the player interface
  • Changes apply immediately throughout the application
  • Add missing Language: Go to the folder where you installed the app, then navigate to the "translations" folder. Pick one of the existing translation files of your choice, translate it using a translator or AI, and save it within the folder with a custom name (such as "custom" or any name you prefer). The next time you start the app, you will find your new language available.
    • Step 1: For example, open or copy "en.json" and go to an AI tool like Grok, ChatGPT, or any other. Paste the content and ask: "Translate this into Bengali in JSON format"
    • Step 2: Once the translation is finished, copy the code. Open your text editor (search for Notepad, Text Editor, or Mousepad) and paste the code
    • Step 3: Press Ctrl+S or go to File then Save. Use a name like "custom.json" or "bengali.json" and save it in the "translations" folder. Done!

Theme & Appearance

  • Theme Name: Choose from built-in themes in the Appearance section. Click the button to view the theme mode
  • Theme Mode: Select between Auto, Light, Dark mode, or Custom theme
  • Button Accent Color: Customize the accent color for buttons and UI elements (default: #3584e4)
  • Background Image: Set a custom background image for the player interface
  • Background Enabled: Toggle custom background on/off
  • Custom Themes Directory: Specify a folder containing your custom CSS theme files, default one is where you installed the application at "themes"
  • Activating Custom Themes:
    • Step 1: Click on "Custom Theme" to activate another row of buttons
    • Step 2: You will see a button labeled Adwaita (GTK)
    • Step 3: Click that button and you will see Custom Theme option
    • Step 4: If you have defined a directory, you will find a list of your available themes
  • Theme Sample: Click this Themes link to get samples. You can edit them to match your vibe
  • Custom themes allow you to change the appearance of almost everything, including fonts, lyrics display, buttons (such as play/pause), animations (like how the left panel opens), and much more

TROUBLESHOOT: Theme changing from custom to system like "Light or DARK" sometimes fails, so just scroll settings to down and click "Reset to Defaults" Button.

Library & File Management

  • Last Opened Folder: View the most recently accessed folder path
  • Recent Folders: Access your 10 most recently opened folders
  • Clear Recent Folders: Remove all folders from the recent list
  • Hidden Folders: Manage folders that should be excluded from library scanning
  • Add Hidden Folder: Specify folders to skip during media scanning.
  • Remove Hidden Folder: Remove folders from the hidden list to include them in scans.
  • Clear All Hidden Folders: Reset the hidden folders list.

AI Features

  • AI Widget: Enable or disable the AI assistant widget.
  • When enabled, you can use AI to search and play media with voice or text commands.

Technical Settings

  • Media Backend: Choose between available media backends (default: VLC) Do not change if you want for AI to choose itself.
  • Different backends may offer better compatibility with certain file formats
  • Changing the backend may require restarting the application

Settings Management

  • Auto-Save: All settings are automatically saved when changed
  • Settings Location: Configuration stored in ~/.player/settings.json (Linux) or similar location on your system
  • Reset to Defaults: Restore all settings to factory defaults if needed
  • Settings Validation: The player automatically validates and repairs corrupted settings files

About Information

The settings panel also displays application information including:

  • Application name and version number
  • Developer information and credits
  • Feedback email for support and suggestions
  • Application description and features overview

Tips for Configuring Settings

  • Use hidden folders to exclude system directories or folders with non-media files from scanning
  • Custom themes must be CSS files placed in your designated custom themes directory
  • Auto mode for theme automatically switches between light and dark based on system preferences
  • Recent folders help you quickly return to frequently accessed media locations
  • Experiment with different media backends if you experience playback issues with certain formats

Playback Controls

Take complete control over your media playback experience with 4allMedia Player's comprehensive control system, featuring basic and advanced playback options, context menus, and top navigation tools.

Bottom Control Bar

The bottom control bar offers two viewing modes for different user preferences:

Minimalist Mode (Default)

By default, the control bar displays only essential controls:

  • Play/Pause Button: Toggle playback with a single click
  • Progress Bar: Visual timeline with hover preview showing time stamps
  • Volume Control: Adjust audio level
  • Toggle Button: Show or hide extended controls
  • Hamburger Menu: Access recently played files

Extended Controls Mode

Click the toggle button to reveal additional playback controls:

Basic Playback Buttons:

  • ▶️ Play: Start media playback
  • ⏸️ Pause: Pause current media
  • ⏹️ Stop: Stop playback completely
  • ⏭️ Next: Skip to next track in playlist
  • ⏮️ Previous: Go back to previous track

Advanced Control Buttons (Extended Mode)

When you toggle to extended mode, additional control buttons appear providing quick access to advanced features:

  • Shuffle Button: Randomize playback order for variety
  • Repeat Button: Cycle through Repeat All, Repeat One, or Repeat Off modes
  • A-B Repeat Button: Loop specific sections of media for practice or review
  • AR (Aspect Ratio) Button: Quickly change video aspect ratio
  • Fullscreen Button: Toggle fullscreen mode on/off
  • Picture-in-Picture Button: Enable mini player mode
  • Equalizer Button: Open 10-band EQ with preset options for customized audio
  • AI Button: Access AI assistant features
  • Volume Button: Fine-tune audio levels and access audio boost (up to 100%; Ultra Boost 200% coming soon)
  • Subtitle Button: Load and configure subtitle files

These buttons provide instant access to commonly used features without opening menus, streamlining your playback experience.

Playing Area - Right-Click Context Menu

Right-click anywhere on the playing media area to access a comprehensive context menu with advanced features:

File Operations

  • Open File: Browse and open media files from your system
  • Open URL: Play streams and online media by pasting URLs

Audio Menu

  • Audio Track: Select from multiple audio tracks in the media file
  • Audio Visualization: Toggle visual effects synchronized with audio
  • Audio Device: Choose your output device (speakers, headphones, etc.)
  • Lyrics Overlay: Display synchronized lyrics (coming soon)
  • Stereo Mode: Choose from Stereo, Mono, Left Only, Right Only, or Reverse Stereo

Video Menu

  • Video Track: Select from multiple video tracks
  • Zoom: Adjust video size (0.5x, 1x, 1.5x, 2x)
  • Screen Rotation: Rotate video by 0°, 90°, 180°, or 270°
  • Crop Ratios: Select from multiple aspect ratios including:
    • Standard formats: 16:10, 16:9, 4:3, 5:4, 1:1
    • Cinematic formats: 1.85:1, 2.21:1, 2.35:1, 2.39:1
  • Deinterlace: Toggle deinterlacing for better video quality
  • Deinterlace Modes: Choose from Discard, Blend, Mean, Bob, Linear, X, Yadif, or Yadif (2x)

Playback Menu

  • Speed Control: Precise playback speed adjustment
    • Super Slow (0.25x), Slow (0.5x), Normal (1.0x)
    • Fast (1.5x), Super Fast (2.0x), Ultra Fast (3.0x)
  • Chapters: View and navigate chapter markers in the media
  • Next/Previous Chapter: Quick chapter navigation
  • Jump to Time: Seek to a specific timestamp
  • Render: Export or render media segments
  • Record: Capture live playback
  • Bookmarks: Manage saved positions in your media

Other Menu

  • Play YouTube Video: Direct YouTube playback support
  • Add URL: Add streaming URLs to your playlist
  • VR Mode: Enable VR glasses support for immersive viewing
  • Play 360° Video: Activate 3D/VR mode for 360-degree videos
  • Play Blu-ray Disk: Direct Blu-ray disc playback
  • Media Backend: Switch between GStreamer, MPV, or VLC backends for optimal compatibility

Top Navigation Bar

The top navigation bar provides quick access to essential functions and information:

  • Back Button: Exit playback and return to library view
  • Stats Button: View detailed statistics of your viewing activity and media info
  • H Button: Hide/show the left panel during playback for quick access to your library without leaving the player
  • File Name Display: Shows the currently playing media file name
  • Information Button: View detailed media properties including file size, audio codec, video codec, resolution, frame rate, bitrate, and more
  • Screenshot Button: Capture still images from video playback (not available for audio-only files)
  • T Button: Toggle visibility of the top navigation bar; automatically hides after 5 seconds during video playback for an immersive viewing experience
